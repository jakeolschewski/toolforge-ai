# Professional Bio & Profile Writer - Quality Control Checklist

## Section 1: Factual Accuracy
- [ ] All job titles match the subject's actual current and past positions
- [ ] Company names are spelled correctly and use official formatting
- [ ] Dates of employment and tenure claims are verified
- [ ] Educational credentials (degrees, institutions, years) are accurate
- [ ] Quantitative achievements (revenue figures, percentages, team sizes) are confirmed
- [ ] Certifications and professional affiliations are current and valid

## Section 2: Content Completeness
- [ ] Bio includes a clear statement of current role and responsibility
- [ ] At least one quantifiable achievement is present in every version
- [ ] Educational background is included where appropriate for the platform
- [ ] A unique differentiator or personal element is included
- [ ] Call to action or next step is present (explicit or implicit)
- [ ] All required bio versions (per platform list) have been drafted

## Section 3: Tone & Voice Calibration
- [ ] Tone matches the target platform (formal for corporate, conversational for social)
- [ ] Person perspective is correct (third person for formal, first person for social)
- [ ] Language complexity is appropriate for the target audience
- [ ] Brand voice guidelines have been followed (if applicable)
- [ ] Bio reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] No awkward transitions between career highlights and personal elements

## Section 4: Length & Format Compliance
- [ ] Each bio version meets its target word/character count
- [ ] LinkedIn summary is under 2,600 characters
- [ ] Twitter/X bio is under 160 characters
- [ ] Conference bios match the event's specified length requirement
- [ ] Paragraphs are appropriately sized for digital reading (3-4 sentences max)
- [ ] Formatting is correct for the deployment platform (plain text, markdown, HTML)

## Section 5: Language Quality
- [ ] No spelling or grammatical errors
- [ ] Active voice is used throughout (no passive constructions)
- [ ] Cliches and buzzwords are eliminated or substantiated with specifics
- [ ] Sentences average 15-20 words (not overly complex)
- [ ] No jargon that the target audience would not understand
- [ ] Verb choices are strong and varied (not repetitive)
- [ ] Acronyms are spelled out on first use where appropriate

## Section 6: Cross-Version Consistency
- [ ] Core professional identity statement is consistent across all versions
- [ ] No factual contradictions between different bio lengths
- [ ] Achievement rankings are logical (most important first in shorter versions)
- [ ] Name, title, and company are identical across all versions
- [ ] Tone progression is coherent (longer versions expand, not contradict, shorter ones)
- [ ] Master bio document has been updated with all final versions

## Section 7: Privacy & Sensitivity Review
- [ ] No private contact information (personal phone, home address) is included
- [ ] Sensitive affiliations or memberships are included only with subject approval
- [ ] Salary or compensation details are excluded
- [ ] References to health, family, or personal matters are authorized by the subject
- [ ] Redaction Guide has been consulted and applied
- [ ] Subject has given explicit approval to publish all included information

## Section 8: Platform Optimization
- [ ] LinkedIn bio includes relevant industry keywords for search visibility
- [ ] Website bio is formatted with appropriate headings or sections
- [ ] Conference bio emphasizes topic-relevant expertise
- [ ] Social media bios use high-impact language within character limits
- [ ] Internal directory bio includes collaboration-relevant information
- [ ] SEO considerations are addressed for publicly indexed bios

## Section 9: Subject Approval & Deployment
- [ ] Draft has been sent to the subject for review
- [ ] Subject feedback has been incorporated
- [ ] Final versions are approved in writing (email or message confirmation)
- [ ] Bios are saved in the correct format for each deployment platform
- [ ] Old bio versions have been archived (not deleted)
- [ ] Publication dates and next review dates are recorded

## Section 10: Maintenance Planning
- [ ] Quarterly review date is set in calendar
- [ ] Master bio document is stored in an accessible, backed-up location
- [ ] Update triggers are defined (role change, major achievement, company rebrand)
- [ ] Responsibility for future updates is assigned
- [ ] Version history is maintained with dates and change summaries
