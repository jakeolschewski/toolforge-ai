# Quality Control Checklist - Content Idea Generator

## Pre-Ideation Verification

### 1. Audience Research Completeness
- [ ] Target audience segments clearly defined with demographics and psychographics
- [ ] Recent audience feedback or survey data reviewed (within last 30 days)
- [ ] Audience pain points documented with specific examples
- [ ] Content consumption preferences identified (formats, channels, frequency)
- [ ] Existing audience personas referenced and up to date

### 2. Strategic Alignment Check
- [ ] Current business goals documented and prioritized
- [ ] Content pillars confirmed and relevant to this ideation round
- [ ] Seasonal events and campaigns on the calendar accounted for
- [ ] Brand voice and messaging guidelines accessible
- [ ] Key performance metrics defined for content success
- [ ] Budget and resource constraints noted

### 3. Research Input Quality
- [ ] Google Trends or equivalent data pulled for relevant topics
- [ ] Competitor content reviewed (at least 3 competitors, last 30 days)
- [ ] Social media discussions and questions captured
- [ ] Industry news and emerging trends documented
- [ ] Content gaps identified with supporting evidence
- [ ] All research notes organized and accessible

## Ideation Quality Control

### 4. Brainstorming Output Assessment
- [ ] Minimum of 25-30 raw ideas generated per session
- [ ] Ideas span at least 3 different content formats
- [ ] Ideas cover multiple content pillars (not clustered in one area)
- [ ] Both timely and evergreen ideas are represented
- [ ] Contrarian or unique angles included alongside standard approaches
- [ ] No premature filtering occurred during brainstorming phase
- [ ] Ideas are specific enough to be actionable (not vague concepts)

### 5. Scoring Rubric Consistency
- [ ] All ideas scored on the same 1-5 scale across all criteria
- [ ] Audience Relevance scores supported by research data
- [ ] Business Alignment scores tied to documented goals
- [ ] Uniqueness scores reflect actual competitive gap analysis
- [ ] Feasibility scores account for current team capacity and resources
- [ ] Timeliness scores consider publication lead times
- [ ] Composite scores calculated correctly with no arithmetic errors
- [ ] Top ideas clearly distinguished from mid-tier and low-priority ideas

### 6. Content Brief Quality
- [ ] Each selected idea has a 2-3 sentence brief explaining the angle
- [ ] Target audience segment specified for each idea
- [ ] Content format and estimated length assigned
- [ ] Primary goal (awareness, engagement, conversion) identified
- [ ] Suggested keywords or themes noted for SEO consideration
- [ ] No duplicate or near-duplicate ideas in the selected batch

## Post-Ideation Verification

### 7. Calendar Integration
- [ ] Selected ideas mapped to specific editorial calendar dates
- [ ] Publication dates account for production lead time
- [ ] Seasonal and event-driven content scheduled with appropriate buffers
- [ ] No calendar conflicts with existing planned content
- [ ] Dependencies (interviews, data, assets) identified and assigned
- [ ] Calendar shared with all relevant team members

### 8. Backlog Management
- [ ] Unselected ideas archived in a searchable backlog system
- [ ] Backlog ideas tagged by topic, format, and pillar
- [ ] Duplicate ideas in the backlog identified and merged
- [ ] Outdated or irrelevant backlog items flagged for removal
- [ ] Backlog reviewed within the last 90 days

### 9. Privacy and Data Protection
- [ ] No proprietary audience data included in AI prompts
- [ ] Competitor names and strategies redacted from shared documents
- [ ] Internal analytics figures replaced with ranges or percentages
- [ ] No client-specific information exposed in ideation outputs
- [ ] Redaction Guide consulted before sharing ideas externally
- [ ] All AI-generated outputs reviewed for inadvertent data leaks

### 10. Process Improvement
- [ ] Session duration tracked and compared to estimates
- [ ] Number of ideas generated tracked for trend analysis
- [ ] Conversion rate from idea to published content monitored
- [ ] Team feedback on ideation process collected
- [ ] Frameworks and techniques rotated to prevent stagnation
- [ ] Lessons learned documented for next session
