# Examples - Content Idea Generator

## Overview

These examples demonstrate how the Content Idea Generator workflow produces actionable content ideas across different industries and use cases. Each scenario walks through the full process from research to prioritized output.

---

## Example 1: SaaS Company Blog Ideation

**Context:** A B2B SaaS company producing productivity tools needs fresh blog content ideas for Q2. Their audience is mid-level managers at companies with 50-500 employees.

**Audience Alignment:**
- Segment: Operations managers seeking efficiency improvements
- Pain points: Tool sprawl, team adoption resistance, ROI justification
- Content pillars: Productivity tips, tool comparisons, workflow automation, leadership

**Trend Research Findings:**
- Rising interest in "AI-assisted project management"
- Competitor gap: No one covers "change management for tool adoption"
- Social media buzz around "async work culture"

**Top 5 Scored Ideas:**

| Rank | Title | Format | Score |
|------|-------|--------|-------|
| 1 | The Manager's Guide to AI-Assisted Project Management | Long-form guide | 23/25 |
| 2 | Why Your Team Resists New Tools (And How to Fix It) | Blog post | 22/25 |
| 3 | Async vs. Sync: How Top Teams Balance Communication | Comparison post | 21/25 |
| 4 | 7 Signs Your Tech Stack Is Slowing Your Team Down | Listicle | 20/25 |
| 5 | ROI Calculator: Is That New Tool Worth the Switch? | Interactive tool + blog | 19/25 |

**Content Brief (Idea #1):**
A comprehensive guide targeting operations managers who are curious about AI project management tools but unsure where to start. The angle focuses on practical, low-risk ways to integrate AI into existing workflows without disrupting team productivity. Goal: awareness and lead generation.

## Example 2: Freelance Health & Wellness Writer

**Context:** A freelance writer specializing in health and wellness content needs to pitch ideas to multiple publications. They focus on evidence-based wellness for working professionals.

**Audience Alignment:**
- Segment: Busy professionals aged 30-50 interested in sustainable wellness
- Pain points: Information overload, unrealistic fitness advice, work-life balance
- Content pillars: Nutrition, movement, mental health, sleep, workplace wellness

**Question-Stacking Output (Topic: Workplace Wellness):**

*HOW ideas:*
- How to Build a 10-Minute Desk Stretching Routine That Actually Sticks
- How Remote Workers Can Create Boundaries Without Guilt

*WHY ideas:*
- Why Standing Desks Alone Won't Fix Your Posture (What Will)
- Why Your Afternoon Slump Starts at Breakfast

*WHAT ideas:*
- What "Wellness Culture" Gets Wrong About Productivity
- What Science Actually Says About Blue Light Glasses

**Scoring Result:** The contrarian piece "What Wellness Culture Gets Wrong About Productivity" scored highest (24/25) due to strong differentiation, audience relevance, and pitch potential for major publications.

## Example 3: E-Commerce Brand Seasonal Planning

**Context:** An online home goods retailer planning content for the fall/holiday season. Their audience is homeowners aged 28-45 who value design and sustainability.

**Seasonal Triggers:**
- Back-to-school home organization (August-September)
- Fall home refresh and cozy decor (September-October)
- Holiday gifting and entertaining (November-December)

**Hub and Spoke Plan (Pillar: Sustainable Home Living):**

*Hub piece:* "The Complete Guide to a Sustainable Home Refresh This Fall"

*Spoke pieces:*
1. 5 Eco-Friendly Alternatives to Fast Furniture
2. How to Style Thrifted Decor Like a Professional Designer
3. Sustainable Gift Guide: Home Goods That Last a Lifetime
4. Room-by-Room Energy Audit: Save Money and the Planet
5. Interview: How One Family Went Zero-Waste in Their Kitchen

**Calendar Integration:**
- Hub piece scheduled for September 1 (anchor for the season)
- Spoke pieces staggered biweekly through November
- Gift guide timed for November 15 (pre-Black Friday)
- All pieces tagged for social media repurposing via Workflow 26
